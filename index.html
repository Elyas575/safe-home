Free Setup Guide: Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Understand DNS - The Internet's Phonebook

DNS (Domain Name System) is like the internet's phonebook. When you type "google.com" into your browser, DNS translates that into an IP address that computers understand. By controlling which DNS server your network uses, you control which websites can be accessed.

Most people use their ISP's default DNS servers, but you can switch to filtered DNS services that automatically block inappropriate content.

2

3

Configure Your Router

This is the most important step. By changing your router's DNS settings, you protect every device that connects to your Wi-Fi:

  1. Find your router's IP address (usually 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1)
  2. Enter this address in your web browser
  3. Log in with your admin credentials
  4. Find the DNS settings (usually under "Internet" or "WAN" settings)
  5. Replace the existing DNS servers with OpenDNS addresses
  6. Save changes and restart your router

💡 Pro Tip

Can't find your router settings? Search "[your router model] DNS settings" for specific instructions. Most modern routers make this easy to configure.

4

Test Your Setup

After configuring your router, test that the filtering is working:

  1. Connect a device to your Wi-Fi
  2. Try visiting a known adult website
  3. You should see a block page instead of the website
  4. Regular websites should still work normally

If you can still access blocked content, you may need to clear your browser cache or wait a few minutes for DNS changes to propagate.

FAQs: Common Questions Answered

🔧 Filtering Not Working?

Devices using mobile data: DNS filtering only works on your Wi-Fi network. When devices switch to mobile data, they use your carrier's DNS.

Smart DNS/VPN services: These will override your router's DNS settings.

Browser settings: Some browsers have their own DNS settings that override system settings.

Alternative DNS Services

While OpenDNS is our recommended solution, here are other free options:

  • CleanBrowsing: Family-friendly filtering with different security levels
  • Quad9: Focuses on security and malware protection
  • Cloudflare: Fast DNS with optional family filtering
